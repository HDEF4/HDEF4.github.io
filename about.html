Getting to Armagh


THIS INFORMATION IS PROVISIONAL AND SLIGHTLY OUT OF DATE, currently under review
Northern Ireland can be reached by air from many cities in Britain, mainland Europe and beyond, and by ferry from England or Scotland. Belfast has two airports, Belfast International Airport and Belfast City Airport, both are ~40 miles from Armagh. It is also possible to travel to Northern Ireland via Dublin Airport in the Republic of Ireland, which is ~80 miles from Armagh. Note, however, that a separate visa may be required for certain nationals.
From Belfast City or Belfast International Airport
A bus service operates from both airports to Belfast Europa Bus Centre. The Airbus service from Belfast International Airport operates between 05:45 and 23:20. The Airlink service from Belfast City Airport operates between 06:00 and 21:50.
The Ulsterbus Goldline services between Belfast Europa Buscentre and Armagh takes around 1 hour.
From Dublin Airport
The Ulsterbus Goldline X4 service connects Dublin Airport direct to Armagh. There are only a small number of services each way, so it is advisable to consult the timetable before travelling. It is also possible to take a bus from Dublin Airport to Newry (X1 service) and then take a taxi to Armagh.
Northern Ireland Railways and Irish Rail jointly operate an express service between Belfast and Dublin city centre, stopping at Portadown. Portadown Station is ~10 miles from Armagh. It is advisable to take a taxi from Portdown to Armagh as there are limited buses available.
