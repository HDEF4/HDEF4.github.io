Getting to Armagh


THIS INFORMATION IS PROVISIONAL AND SLIGHTLY OUT OF DATE (2014) Northern Ireland can be reached by air from many cities in Britain, mainland Europe and beyond, and by ferry from England or Scotland. There are two ferry ports: Belfast and Larne. Belfast has two airports, Belfast International Airport and Belfast City Airport, both are ~40 miles from Armagh. EasyJet and Jet2.com operate a range of scheduled services to Belfast International Airport, and BMI, Flybe and British Airways operate scheduled services to Belfast City Airport.
A bus service operates from both airports to Belfast Europa Bus Centre. The Airbus service from Belfast International Airport operates between 05:45 and 23:20. The Airlink service from Belfast City Airport operates between 06:00 and 21:50.
It is also possible to travel to Northern Ireland via Dublin Airport in the Republic of Ireland, which is ~80 miles from Armagh. Note, however, that a separate visa may be required for certain nationals.
The Ulsterbus express bus between Belfast Europa Buscentre and Armagh takes about 1 hour. Avoid the regular service which takes the pretty route, stops everywhere and takes a long time. It is possible to take a bus from Dublin Airport to Newry and then take a taxi to Armagh.
Northern Ireland Railways operate an express service between Belfast and Dublin, stopping at Portadown. Portadown Station is ~10 miles from Armagh. It is advisable to take a taxi from Portdown to Armagh as there are limited buses available.
Translink - Northern Ireland's integrated bus and rail public transport company. http://www.translink.co.uk
Bus Éireann - National bus company in Republic of Ireland http://www.buseireann.ie
Aircoach - An airport coach service form Belfast to Dublin Airport via Newry http://www.aircoach.ie
