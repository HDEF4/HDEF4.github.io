Getting to Armagh

Northern Ireland can be reached by air from many cities in Britain, mainland Europe and beyond. The nearest major airports to Armagh are in Belfast and Dublin (Republic of Ireland). Belfast has two airports, Belfast International Airport and Belfast City Airport, both are ~40 miles from Armagh and are well connected with major UK and European airports. It is also possible to travel to Armagh via Dublin Airport in the Republic of Ireland, which is well connected to many major European airports, Dublin is ~80 miles from Armagh. Note, however, that a separate visa may be required for certain nationals. While further from Armagh, a direct (but infrequent) connection exists between Armagh and Dublin Airport, unlike either of the Belfast airports.

From Belfast City Airport or Belfast International Airport

A bus service operates from both airports to Belfast Europa Bus Centre. The Translink 300 service from Belfast International Airport operates between 05:00 and 00:00 and takes between 30 and 45 minutes to reach Belfast. The Translink 600 service from Belfast City Airport operates between 05:30 (07:30 on Sundays) and 21:50 and takes about 15 minutes. A rail service operates from Sydenham station (~10 minute walk from City Airport) to Great Victoria Street station, adjacent to the Europa Bus Centre, taking 15 minutes.

Translink Goldline services between Belfast Europa Buscentre and Armagh (250/251/270/271) take between 1 and 1.5 hours. Services typically operate between 09:00 and 20:00, although the frequency and operating hours of these services are less at weekends. Please consult the bus timetables on the Translink website, or use the Translink Journey Planner before travelling.

Alternatively, Northern Ireland Railways operate rail services between Belfast city centre and Portadown. Portadown Railway Station is ~10 miles from Armagh. It is advisable to take a taxi from Portdown to Armagh as there are limited buses available.

From Dublin Airport

The Translink X4 service connects Dublin Airport direct to Armagh. There are only a small number of services each way, so it is advisable to consult the timetable before travelling. It is also possible to take a bus from Dublin Airport to Newry (X1 service) and then take a taxi to Armagh.

Northern Ireland Railways and Irish Rail jointly operate the Enterprise express service between Belfast and Dublin city centre, stopping at Portadown. Portadown Railway Station is ~10 miles from Armagh. The timetable can be found here. It is advisable to take a taxi from Portdown to Armagh as there are limited buses available.
